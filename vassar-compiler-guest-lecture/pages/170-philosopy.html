On the difficulty of communication



















I started talking about the need and usefulness of compilers. The focus of this talk has been about how we can make a computer or machine "talk" communicate back to a human in a way that is easier for a human to understand. Personally, I spend a lot of time with computers and a lot of time trying to figure out and fix computer bugs, a big part of that is why a computer is not behaving in the way I would like it to.

In closing, I'd like to reflect on this a little this. This part is a bit whimsical and philosophical.

A computer is a human-made object. We decide how it should be built, and we design what instructions it should perform. But further, we also design programming languages or how we want to instruct or communicate with a computer to do our service. We design a translator from to bridge the gap from human thought to computer action. And yet, when there is a misunderstanding, we spend a great deal of our time and effort in trying to figure out what went wrong and why the computer is performing the way it is and how we can adjust this behavior by adjusting the instructions we give it.

To me, this demonstrates a deeper problem of the difficultly in communication. Al Aho once remarked that he felt that in contrast to programming languages which we teach should use "context free" or unambiguous grammars, human language is possibly inherently ambiguous. Possibly because we do not want others to know exactly we mean.

But that kind of introduction of confusion is not what is going on here.

In the 1960's or so there was an effort to try to look for messages from extra-terrestrial beings, or send messages out into space so that they might be able to recognize us. From what I have encountered in my day-to-day experience in communicating with computers and trying to understand their communication back, I think the chances of success here are very slim.

Still, I personally have spent a careers worth of interest and effort in trying to make computers communicate back to humans in a slightly better and more human-friendly way. I hope today I have shed a little light on what that endeavor looks like.
